## The question

Harper's stated mantra: *own the moment, don't let the moment own you* — and, this year, take the walks and cut back on chase. This product tests that claim where it actually lives: the shadow band, one ball width (±0.25 ft) on either side of the rulebook zone. Takes first, then the punishment. Is he laying off the pitch just off the edge, and is he still hurting the same pitch when it creeps a ball's width onto the plate?

## Headline

**He is owning the edge — by swinging less and hitting it harder when he does.** The edge-take rate (OZ-1, .411) is his best in five years, the walk rate (14.2%) his best since 2020, and damage on borderline strikes (OZ-4 xwOBAcon .458, barrel 12.1%) snapped back to 2021 levels after two down years. He has not "totally cut back on chase" — 33.7% is better than 2025's 35.6% but level with 2024 — because the improvement is concentrated exactly where it matters: on the edge and in the waste zone, not uniformly. The overall line (.260/.360/.497, .369 wOBA, 20 HR) undersells a profile that is elite against right-handed pitching and carrying one real flaw.

Read: after four seasons stuck near .39, he is taking the borderline ball again (.411, most since 2021). The attack rate on borderline *strikes* also fell (.688, lowest since 2021) — so the raw decision differential (OZ-3) is a tenure low. That is not indecision; it is selectivity. Waste-zone swing rate dropped to .264 (from .289), two-strike edge battle is intact (.887 shadow-in attack with two strikes), and the swings he does take on the edge produce the most damage since the MVP year. Fewer edge swings, much better ones.

## 2 — The punishment

Heart of the zone, 2026: .803 swing rate, **.134 whiff** (best of tenure), **.584 xwOBAcon and .200 barrel rate** — within range of 2021's peak (.618 / .250) and up from 2025 (.541 / .204). When the edge pitch misses fat, he is not missing it.

The 2024–2026 barrel tier (.107–.123) sits clearly below 2019–2023 (.128–.181), and 2026's top-end EV (113.5 max, 106.7 EV90) continues the gentle top-end slide. But the .165 breaking-ball barrel rate is his best since 2021, while the fastball barrel rate (.092) is a tenure low. The old profile beat velocity; the 2026 profile hunts spin. That is consistent with the edge story: breaking balls live on the edges, and the OZ-4 recovery is substantially breaking-ball damage (2026 edge barrel vs breaking: .190).

## 4 — Platoon splits: the watch item

Vs RHP this is his best half since 2021 (.466 full-season wOBA) — an elite line by any definition, Dombrowski discourse included. Vs LHP it is the softest contact of his tenure (88.0 avg EV, .398 hard-hit, both tenure lows). The edge discipline holds against lefties (OZ-1 .417 vs .407); the damage doesn't (.510 edge xwOBAcon vs LHP is inflated by 24 BIP — small sample). The flaw is contact quality, not approach.

## 5 — Shape of the half

March cold open (.145 wOBA, 22 PA), then three months at .376–.420 wOBA with 6–7 HR each, then a 48-PA July fade (.251) into the break. The nine-time All-Star arrives at his home-park All-Star game with the boom intact — 20 HR, 29.4/600 pace — and a K rate (20.1%) that is his best over a half since 2020.

## Actions by persona

**Manager** — the reverse-platoon caution is over: vs RHP he is the anchor. Against elite LHP starters, the .292 wOBA argues for lineup protection behind him rather than rest — the OBP (.321) still plays.
**Hitting department** — the LHP contact-quality collapse (88.0 EV) is the offseason-narrative risk. Approach is intact against lefties; investigate swing path vs LHP breaking away.
**Harper** — the plan is working where he aimed it: best edge takes in five years, best walk rate since 2020, heart-zone punishment at MVP levels. The barrel engine now runs on spin; that is fine, but the .092 fastball barrel rate is the number that decides whether "elite" is past tense.
**DPO / Content** — OZ-1..OZ-4 are provisional; recommend ratification (specs in 03). Second-half watch: fastball barrel rate, LHP avg EV, July fade vs. fatigue. *Next time, on the Phillies data products: the first half of Zack Wheeler's 2026 — a story of redemption set up for a truly dominant second-half run. At least let's hope so.*
